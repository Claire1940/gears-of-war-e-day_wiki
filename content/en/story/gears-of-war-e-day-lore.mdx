<Section icon={Map} title="Origin of the Locust War: Planet Sera's Darkest Day">

To truly grasp the **gears of war e-day lore**, one must look back to the fragile peace that preceded the nightmare. For nearly eighty years, the human nations of Sera slaughtered each other in the grueling Pendulum Wars. When the Coalition of Ordered Governments (COG) and the Union of Independent Republics (UIR) finally signed a peace treaty, humanity believed the bloodshed was over. Soldiers returned to civilian life, and cities began to rebuild.

Six weeks after the armistice, the ground shattered. The Locust Horde, a subterranean race of mutated, highly coordinated chimeras, launched a planet-wide assault. This was not a slow infiltration but a campaign of rapid dominance. Using emergence holes, the Locust bypassed human defenses entirely, slaughtering civilians and military forces alike with zero discrimination. Within the first twenty-six hours, over a quarter of the human population was killed, and millions went missing.

The Locust were not invading out of simple malice; they were fleeing a parasitic, Imulsion-based infection in the Hollow known as Lambency. Having failed to secure a cure from human scientists, Queen Myrrah ordered the total eradication of humanity to colonize the surface for her people's survival.

<Section icon={Swords} title="The Fall of Kalona: Three Days of Survival Horror">

While Emergence Day was a global catastrophe, the narrative of this prequel focuses heavily on the metropolitan city of Kalona. Once celebrated as a thriving industrial center of scientific innovation and Imulsion production, Kalona becomes the ultimate testing ground for human survival. The entire campaign unfolds over a compact, highly intense three-day timeline, tracking the city's rapid descent into a warzone.

This focused setting allows the story to trade the massive military blockades of later games for a claustrophobic, survival-horror atmosphere. In Kalona, the Locust are not standard video game enemies; they are the "monsters under the bed," terrifying, formidable, and seemingly unstoppable. The campaign's linear, narrative-driven design spans over fourteen hours of gameplay, making it the longest single-player story mode in franchise history.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Bravo Squad and the Bonds of Brotherhood">

At the heart of the narrative is the legendary brotherhood between Marcus Fenix and Dominic Santiago. However, at this point in the timeline, they are not yet the hardened, unstoppable heroes of the original trilogy. They are young, armorless, and deeply traumatized veterans trying to navigate life after the Pendulum Wars. 

Their relationship is strained by shared grief. Both men are mourning the loss of Carlos Santiago—Dom's older brother and Marcus's best friend—who died in combat shortly before the war ended. The emergence forces them to confront their trauma while relying on each other to survive.

To expand the scope of the conflict, the campaign introduces Bravo Squad, a four-member unit that remains the sole perspective of the entire game. By sticking strictly to this squad, the game ensures players only see and learn what the characters do in real-time.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="The Origin of the Chainsaw Lancer">

One of the most anticipated elements of the prequel's lore is the origin of the iconic chainsaw-equipped Lancer assault rifle. During the Pendulum Wars, COG infantry utilized the Retro Lancer, which featured a traditional physical bayonet designed for human-on-human combat. However, the Locust presented a completely different anatomical challenge.

Gears quickly discovered that standard bayonets were highly ineffective against the dense, mineral-rich, and armored hides of the Locust Horde. Blades would frequently dull, snap, or become lodged in the monsters, leaving soldiers completely defenseless. Out of desperate necessity, Marcus Fenix and military engineers co-designed a motorized chainsaw attachment powered by Imulsion.
