<Section icon={Shield} title="Horde Siege: Reimagining 12-Player PvE Co-op">

For players seeking a massive cooperative challenge beyond the main story, Gears of War: E-Day introduces **Horde Siege**. This mode is a significant evolution of the franchise's classic wave-defense gameplay, expanding the PvE battlefield to support up to 12 players simultaneously. Rather than defending a single choke point, players are divided into three distinct four-player squads to defend entire city blocks of Kalona from coordinated Locust assaults.

Success in Horde Siege relies on class synergy, resource management, and squad positioning. The mode features four distinct classes, each designed to handle specific threats on the battlefield. Teams must balance their composition to ensure they have enough offensive power, defensive utility, and healing capabilities to survive the escalating waves.

| Class | Primary Role | Key Weapon | Ability Focus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Assault | Frontline Damage | Gnasher / Lancer | Enemy pressure and close-quarters engagement |
| Marksman | Long-Range Support | Longshot Precision | Holding sightlines and vertical flanking |
| Medic | Squad Survival | Sub-machine gun | Healing, reviving, and defensive buffs |
| Breacher | Fortification Clearer | Gut Puncher / Explosives | Closing E-holes and destroying enemy cover |

Horde Siege maps are much larger than traditional Gears multiplayer arenas. They represent war-torn urban zones, featuring multi-story buildings, industrial waterfronts, and sports complexes. The scale of these maps requires squads to split up to secure secondary objectives, such as ammo caches or fabricator drop points, before regrouping to face massive world bosses that spawn at key intervals.

<Callout type="tip" title="Squad Synergy is Key">
In Horde Siege, ensure at least one Breacher and one Medic are assigned to each active zone. Breachers are essential for quickly closing emerging E-Holes, while Medics keep frontline Assault players on their feet during intense close-range encounters.
</Callout>

To understand how Horde Siege differs from traditional survival modes, players can compare the structural changes introduced in this installment.

| Feature | Classic Horde Mode | Horde Siege PvE |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Total Players | 5 Players | 12 Players |
| Squad Layout | Single 5-player team | Three 4-player squads |
| Map Scale | Medium symmetrical arenas | Large-scale Kalona city districts |
| Objectives | Wave survival and defense | Dynamic city-wide objectives and world bosses |
| Class System | Basic combat roles | Specialized tactical classes with active abilities |

<Section icon={Map} title="Tactical Traversal and Team Combat Strategies">

Combat in Gears of War: E-Day is built around a modernized version of the classic cover-shooter puzzle. In cooperative play, the environment becomes a dynamic tool. The introduction of vertical traversal, smoother cover transitions, and micro-destruction changes how co-op partners must coordinate their movements.

Players can now slide from sprints, climb vertical routes, and jump across gaps to establish high-ground flanking positions. While one player holds the frontline behind low-ground cover with a Gnasher, a co-op partner can scale a nearby balcony with a Longshot to eliminate enemy suppression units.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Establish Suppression and Draw Aggro">
    One player utilizes heavy cover to draw the attention of Locust Drones, using the Lancer to keep enemies pinned behind their own barriers.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Execute Vertical Flanking">
    Co-op partners use the modernized jumping and climbing mechanics to scale exterior ladders or jump across ruined rooftops to secure high-ground sightlines.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Isolate and Eliminate High-Value Targets">
    The flanking players use precision weapons like the Longshot or explosive tools like the Gut Puncher to bypass enemy cover and eliminate high-threat targets.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Secure and Close E-Holes">
    Once the area is cleared of immediate threats, the squad advances together to seal active E-Holes with well-placed grenades before reinforcements can emerge.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Micro-Destruction Mechanics">
Be aware that cover is no longer permanent. Micro-destruction mechanics allow enemy fire to chip away at stone, concrete, and wooden barriers. Co-op partners must constantly communicate and reposition as their defensive positions degrade during prolonged firefights.
</Callout>

Environmental hazards also play a major role in co-op combat. The emergence of sinkholes can swallow entire vehicles and alter the layout of the map in real-time. Squads must remain highly mobile, avoiding static defense strategies in favor of fluid, cover-to-cover transitions.

</Section>
