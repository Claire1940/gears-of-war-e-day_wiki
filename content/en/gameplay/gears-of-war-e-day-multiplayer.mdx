<Section icon={Shield} title="Horde Siege: 12-Player PvE Mechanics">

Horde Siege is the evolution of the franchise's signature wave-survival mode. Instead of the traditional 5-player setup, Horde Siege tasks **12 players** with defending the war-torn city of Kalona. The player base is divided into three distinct 4-player squads, which can choose to stick together to hold a single choke point or split up to secure multiple defensive perimeters across the map.

This mode emphasizes class synergy and resource management. Players must choose from four confirmed classes, each featuring unique passive abilities, loadouts, and ultimate skills that can turn the tide of a massive Locust assault.

### Confirmed Horde Siege Classes

| Class | Primary Weapon | Role | Unique Utility |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Assault** | Chainsaw Lancer | Frontline damage dealer | Increased ammo capacity, explosive buffs |
| **Marksman** | Longshot | Long-range precision | High-damage critical shots, wall-penetrating scans |
| **Medic** | Lancer / Snub Pistol | Squad healing & survival | Area-of-effect healing beacons, fast revives |
| **Breacher** | Gnasher / Gut Puncher | Close-quarters fortification | Heavy armor shields, defensive barricade repair |

### Wave Structure and World Bosses

Unlike classic Horde modes where enemies spawn from static points, Horde Siege features dynamic emergence events. **E-Holes** can open anywhere on the map, forcing squads to reposition or quickly seal the holes with high-explosive ordnance before being overrun. At milestone waves, massive World Bosses emerge, requiring all three squads to coordinate their fire and utilize vertical flanking routes to target weak spots.

<Section icon={Target} title="Versus PvP: 4v4 Competitive Play">

For competitive players, the **gears of war e-day multiplayer** experience centers around refined 4v4 Versus combat. Symmetrical maps, predictable weapon spawn timers, and tight communication form the foundation of E-Day's competitive play. The Coalition has focused heavily on creating balanced lane designs that reward map control, positioning, and mechanical skill over random encounters.

Competitive play features both **Ranked Play** and **Social Playlists**. Ranked Play is designed for highly structured, skill-matched competition with strict penalties for leaving matches, while Social Playlists offer a more relaxed environment to practice movement mechanics and map layouts.

| Mode / Playlist | Player Format | Matchmaking Style | Primary Focus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Ranked Versus** | 4v4 | Skill-Based (SBMM) | Competitive ranking, strict rulesets, seasonal rewards |
| **Social Versus** | 4v4 | Connection-Prioritized | Casual play, map practice, warm-up matches |
| **Special Events** | Varied (e.g., 2v2 / FFA) | Playlist-Specific | Rotating game modes, unique weapon modifiers |

### Modernized Traversal and Combat Movement

Movement is the defining skill gap in competitive Gears of War, and E-Day introduces several modernizations that expand tactical options without sacrificing the weight and impact of the classic cover-to-cover combat loop.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Weapons and Combat Customization">

The combat sandbox of Gears of War: E-Day blends iconic, fan-favorite weaponry with newly introduced tools designed for brutal, close-quarters encounters. Because E-Day is an origin story set 14 years before the original game, players will witness the battlefield birth of signature weapons like the Chainsaw Lancer, which was kit-bashed out of desperation during the first hours of the Locust invasion.

Understanding the optimal range, reload timing, and combat role of each weapon is essential for securing map control in both PvE and PvP environments.

| Weapon | Category | Combat Role | Optimal Range |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Gnasher** | Shotgun | Close-range combat, wall-bounce fights | Point-blank to Short |
| **Lancer (Retro/Classic)**| Assault Rifle | Mid-range suppression, chainsaw melee | Medium |
| **Longshot** | Sniper Rifle | Precision pick-offs, holding sightlines | Long |
| **Gut Puncher** | Explosive Launcher | Armor-piercing splash damage, cover flushing | Medium |
| **Incinerator** | Imulsion Shotgun | Area denial, high-damage close-range bursts | Short |

### Active Reload and Weapon Spawns

The classic Active Reload mechanic returns, rewarding perfectly timed reloads with faster weapon cycling and temporary performance boosts. In Versus matches, powerful power weapons like the Longshot and Gut Puncher do not spawn in player loadouts; instead, they spawn at neutral locations on the map at set intervals, forcing both teams to fight for weapon control.

The campaign and multiplayer showcase the early, improvised versions of the Chainsaw Lancer. These early models feature a more rugged, battlefield-engineered aesthetic, reflecting the desperation of Bravo Squad during the initial three days of the Kalona invasion.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Map} title="Open Beta Access and System Requirements">

To ensure network stability and balance ahead of the official launch on **October 6, 2026**, The Coalition is hosting a multiplayer Open Beta from **August 6 to August 10, 2026**. Participating in the beta allows players to test the matchmaking infrastructure, provide feedback on class balance, and earn exclusive cosmetic rewards that carry over to the launch version of the game.

### How to Secure Open Beta Access

- **Digital Pre-orders**: Purchasing any edition digitally on the Xbox Store or Steam grants automatic entitlement to the Open Beta. No code entry is required.
- **Physical Pre-orders**: Players who pre-order a physical edition through participating retailers will receive a single-use beta access code via email or printed receipt.
- **Game Pass Members**: Game Pass Ultimate and PC Game Pass subscribers receive day-one access to the full game at launch, but early beta access remains tied to pre-order rules.
