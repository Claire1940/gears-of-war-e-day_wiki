### 1. Gears of War: E-Day
Set fourteen years before the original game, this prequel follows young veterans Marcus Fenix and Dominic Santiago as they return from the Pendulum Wars. Instead of finding peace, they witness the ground shatter in the city of Kalona. Over a brutal three-day struggle, they form Bravo Squad alongside new allies Mags Carter and Lucas Reyes to survive the first contact with an unknown subterranean horror.

### 2. Gears of War: Judgment
Taking place just one month after Emergence Day, this entry focuses on Kilo Squad, led by Damon Baird and Augustus Cole. The narrative is framed around a military trial where the squad is accused of treason for using unauthorized technology to save Halvo Bay from the Locust commander Karn.

### 3. Gears Tactics
Set one year after E-Day, this turn-based strategy spin-off follows Gabe Diaz, father of Kait Diaz. Gabe recruits and commands a squad of survivors to hunt down Ukkon, the genetic mastermind responsible for creating the monstrous beasts of the Locust Horde.

### 4. Gears of War / Gears of War: Ultimate Edition
Fourteen years after the ground opened, Marcus Fenix is broken out of prison by Dominic Santiago. Joined by Delta Squad, they embark on a high-stakes mission to secure and detonate the Lightmass Bomb deep within the Locust Hollow to wipe out the enemy threat.

### 5. Gears of War 2
Six months after the Lightmass Bomb failed to end the war, the Coalition of Ordered Governments (COG) makes a final, desperate push. Delta Squad deploys directly into the subterranean Hollow to take the fight directly to the Locust Queen, while searching for Dom's missing wife, Maria.

### 6. Gears of War 3
With the COG dissolved and a new Lambent infection threatening both human and Locust survivors, Marcus Fenix and the remaining members of Delta Squad make a final stand. They must protect a weapon designed by Marcus's father, Adam Fenix, which promises to end the war once and for all.

### 7. Gears of War 4
Twenty-five years after the end of the Locust War, a new generation of heroes emerges. Marcus's son, JD Fenix, alongside his friends Kait Diaz and Del Walker, must go rogue from the reformed COG to defend their homes against a mysterious new threat known as the Swarm.

### 8. Gears 5
As the Swarm war escalates, Kait Diaz journeys across Sera to uncover the truth behind her family's connection to the Locust Horde. This entry features expanded, semi-open world areas and a dramatic narrative choice that reshapes the future of the franchise.

<Section icon={Target} title="Where Gears of War: E-Day Fits in the Timeline">

Gears of War: E-Day is not a spin-off; it is a mainline prequel that serves as a foundational origin story for the entire franchise. By focusing on the first three days of the Locust invasion in the city of Kalona, it strips away the established military infrastructure of the later games and replaces it with raw, apocalyptic survival.

To understand the atmosphere of E-Day, it helps to look at the immediate transition of Sera from a brief period of peace to sudden, absolute chaos.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The Illusion of Peace">
    Following the end of the brutal, decades-long Pendulum Wars, humanity celebrates a fragile armistice. Young veterans Marcus Fenix and Dominic Santiago are in Kalona for a memorial service, carrying the heavy grief of losing Carlos Santiago, Dom's brother and Marcus's best friend.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Ground Shatters">
    Without warning, massive Emergency Holes open across Kalona. The Locust Horde emerges from the subterranean depths of Sera. The first encounters are characterized by terror and confusion, as humanity has no knowledge of these creatures and no weapons designed to fight them.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Bravo Squad Forms">
    In the immediate aftermath, Marcus and Dom unite with Mags Carter, an industrial worker, and Lucas Reyes, a young communications cadet. Together, they form Bravo Squad, fighting to secure key districts of Kalona, including the Imulsion refinery, waterfront, and local airfield.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Collapse of Kalona">
    Over three days, the scale of the invasion becomes undeniable. The defenders realize this is not a localized terrorist attack, but a coordinated planetary war. The campaign concludes as Kalona falls, setting the stage for the fourteen-year struggle leading into the original Gears of War.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### The Evolution of Cover and Traversal
Early Gears games treated cover as static, indestructible blocks. By the time of **Gears of War: E-Day**, the battlefield is highly dynamic. 

The introduction of **micro-destruction** means that stone and wooden cover will chip away under sustained gunfire, forcing players to constantly reposition. Additionally, E-Day introduces **jumping and vertical traversal** for the first time, allowing players to scale rooftops, balconies, and flanking routes to attack the Locust from above.
